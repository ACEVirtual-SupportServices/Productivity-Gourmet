What This Virtual Assistant Service Actually Involves

You may have tools. You may even have half-built automations. You are still doing everything yourself — because none of it is set up around how your specific business actually runs.

I come in, assess what is breaking your flow, and fix it. Research, analysis, documentation, task management, process design, and automation — built around your business, not borrowed from a template. Where AI tools produce faster and more accurate results, I use them. Where your operations require human judgment and discretion, that is what you get.

If you have no existing systems yet, that is not a problem. We build them together. I am experienced working with business owners who are new to delegation, and I approach that with the patience it deserves — not as a limitation but as part of the work.

The goal is an operation that holds up when you step away from it — for an hour, a day, or a week — without anything unravelling behind you.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does virtual assistant support from Productivity Gourmet actually involve?

It depends on where your business is and what it needs. I start with an honest assessment of what is breaking down operationally, then build support around that — research, task management, documentation, process design, automation, and general operational oversight. It is not a fixed menu. It is built around you.

What is the difference between the VA packages and the Executive VA packages?

The standard VA packages — The Quick Cup, The Daily Grind, and The Cold Brew — are built for business owners who need reliable, capable task and operational support. The Executive VA packages — The Sous Chef and The Executive Chef — are for founders and executives who need a high-trust operational partner operating with real authority: someone who manages systems, makes judgment calls, communicates on their behalf, and proactively identifies issues before being asked. The scope, the relationship, and the level of access are fundamentally different.

How is The Cold Brew different from The Daily Grind?

The Daily Grind gives you 20 hours of consistent monthly support — solid for ongoing operational needs. The Cold Brew gives you 15 hours, but your tasks are always handled first with a guaranteed faster turnaround and dedicated focus. It is for clients who cannot afford to wait, where speed and priority access matter more than raw hours.

What is The Extra Course add-on?

The Extra Course is a structured 20-hour monthly block available exclusively to Executive VA retainer clients. It is elected in advance and paid alongside your retainer — it reserves that additional capacity specifically for your business. Unused hours carry over once and expire at the end of the following month. It is not ad-hoc overage; it is a committed extension of your retainer.

Is the $47/hr rate available to standard VA clients?

No. The $47/hr rate applies exclusively to Executive VA retainer clients who need additional hours beyond what is included in their package. Standard VA packages carry their own overage rate of $35/hr.

Do you create social media content or graphics?

No. I do not provide content creation or graphic design. I can publish pre-approved content on your behalf, but content strategy, copywriting, and design are outside this service. If you need someone to manage your social media inboxes — DMs, comments, brand enquiries — that is a separate service I offer under Social Media Inbox Management.

How do you use AI in your work?

I use AI tools for research, analysis, generating multiple perspectives on a problem, building automations, and speeding up documentation. It means faster turnaround without sacrificing accuracy. Everything that touches your sensitive business operations still has human judgment behind it.

What does getting started look like?

You fill out the enquiry form, we schedule a Fit Call to make sure there is a genuine match, I send a proposal, you sign a service agreement, and the first month begins with a 50% deposit. From the second month onward it is full payment. No surprises.

How do you handle confidential business information?

Carefully and deliberately. I use a password manager, always push for shared access over direct password sharing, and I never work from a mobile device — so your business information is only ever handled in a secure, controlled environment. A service agreement covering confidentiality is signed before work begins.

What timezone do you work in? Will that affect me?

I work remotely and asynchronously, which means your location and mine rarely matter. Tasks are turned around without requiring us to be online simultaneously. If you prefer real-time collaboration in your timezone, I accommodate that too.

What happens if you are unavailable?

I communicate ahead of time and provide alternatives. You are never left without notice or a plan.

Can I start with The Quick Cup and move to a retainer later?

Yes. Hourly support is a sensible way to test the working relationship before committing to a retainer. Many clients make that transition once they see how the support fits their workflow.

Do you work with clients who have no existing systems or processes?

Yes, and it is some of the most satisfying work I do. If you are new to delegation or building from scratch, we figure out the structure together. Starting without systems is not a disadvantage — it just means we build the right ones from the ground up.
